Borscht: 
A hearty beet soup originating from Ukraine, popular throughout Russia. Made with beets, cabbage, potatoes, and often served with a dollop of sour cream.

Pelmeni: 
Small dumplings filled with a savory mixture of minced meat, typically beef, pork, or a combination. Boiled and served with melted butter or sour cream.

Olivier Salad: 
A classic Russian salad composed of diced boiled potatoes, carrots, peas, pickles, and bologna mixed with mayonnaise, creating a creamy and flavorful dish.

Beef Stroganoff: 
Tender strips of beef cooked in a creamy sauce with mushrooms and onions, often served over a bed of buttered noodles or rice.

Blini: 
Thin pancakes made from buckwheat or wheat flour, served with various toppings such as sour cream, caviar, jam, or smoked salmon.

Piroshki: 
Flaky pastries filled with a variety of ingredients such as meat, potatoes, cabbage, or cheese, then baked or fried to golden perfection.

Golubtsy: 
Cabbage rolls stuffed with a mixture of ground meat, rice, and onions, simmered in a tomato-based sauce until tender and flavorful.

Solyanka: 
A hearty and tangy soup featuring a mix of meats such as beef, pork, and sausage, along with pickles, olives, and a squeeze of lemon for a distinctive flavor.

Syrniki: 
Pan-fried pancakes made from cottage cheese, eggs, and flour, resulting in a crispy exterior and a soft, creamy interior, often served with sour cream or jam.

Kvass: 
A traditional fermented beverage made from rye bread, water, and sugar, flavored with fruits or herbs. Refreshing and slightly tangy, it's enjoyed as a summer drink or used in soups and marinades.
